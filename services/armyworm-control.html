Stop Armyworms Before They Destroy Your Lawn

West Tennessee lawns are particularly vulnerable in late summer and early fall when armyworm moth flights peak. Knowing what to look for — and acting fast — is the difference between a recovered lawn and a dead one.

Emergency
Emergency

Emergency Armyworm Treatment

Armyworm damage escalates hourly. If you're seeing brown patches appear overnight, ragged grass edges, or birds feeding heavily in your lawn — you likely have armyworms and every hour matters.

We offer priority scheduling for active armyworm infestations. Our treatment stops feeding damage fast so your lawn has the best chance of a full recovery.

  • Priority scheduling for active infestations
  • Fast-acting treatment applied same or next day
  • Stops feeding damage immediately
  • Effective against all instars (larval stages)
  • Follow-up assessment included

Prevention
Prevention

Early Detection Monitoring

The best time to treat armyworms is before the damage is visible. Larvae are most vulnerable when small — treating at the first sign of activity is dramatically more effective than waiting.

Our early detection visits during peak armyworm season check for moth activity, egg masses, and early-instar larvae before they have a chance to spread.

  • Inspections during late summer / early fall peak season
  • Check for egg masses and early larvae
  • Soap-flush testing to detect subsurface activity
  • Perimeter monitoring near wooded and field edges
  • Immediate treatment if activity detected

Barrier
Barrier

Perimeter Defense Spray

Armyworms move in "armies" — migrating from nearby fields, roadsides, and neighboring lawns. A perimeter defense spray creates a barrier zone that kills armyworms as they attempt to enter your lawn.

Especially effective for properties near agricultural fields or wooded areas where armyworm migrations originate, perimeter sprays buy critical time before larvae can reach the center of your turf.

  • Applied along lawn perimeter and entry points
  • Targets migrating armyworm populations
  • Creates protective barrier zone
  • Pairs with full-lawn treatment during heavy infestations
  • Timed to peak migration windows

Lawn Recovery Program

After armyworms are eliminated, your lawn needs help recovering. Depending on the extent of damage, recovery may involve fertilization, overseeding, or soil amendments to help turf fill back in.

We assess the damage after treatment and build a recovery plan tailored to your grass type — so your lawn comes back as quickly and fully as possible.

  • Post-treatment lawn damage assessment
  • Fertilization to support regrowth
  • Overseeding recommendations for heavily damaged areas
  • Irrigation and care guidance
  • Progress check-in 3–4 weeks after treatment
